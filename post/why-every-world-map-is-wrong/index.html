Why every world map is wrong

By Pedro Mas Buitrago

January 4, 2018

Have you ever wondered how is it possible to represent the Earth on a 2D map? If you haven´t, now is the time.

In fact, every map of the world you have looked at is wrong, or rather distorted. This is the result of the Mercator projection, which is famous for its ability to represent loxodromes (lines crossing all meridians at a constant angle) as straight segments. Objects are distorted because this projection deforms the distances between meridians into parallel lines, increasing their real widht more and more as it approaches the poles, and also enlarges the distance between parallels as it gets closer to the poles. In this video, you can see perfectly how the Mercator projection works.

The main result of this projection is that land masses near the poles appear much larger than they really are relative to those near the equator. For example, on all world maps you see that North America is bigger than Africa, but in reality Africa is three times bigger!
